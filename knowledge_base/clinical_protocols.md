## PROTOCOL 1: Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP) — Outpatient Management

**Applicable to**: Adults ≥18 years presenting to vClinic with CAP symptoms

### Diagnosis Criteria
- Clinical: fever/chills, productive cough, dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain
- Radiologic: new pulmonary infiltrate on chest X-ray
- Exclude: hospital-acquired, healthcare-associated, aspiration pneumonia

### Risk Stratification (PSI/PORT Score — required for all CAP)
| PSI Class | Score | Recommendation |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| I | N/A (age <50, no comorbidities, normal exam) | Outpatient |
| II | ≤70 | Outpatient |
| III | 71–90 | Outpatient or brief observation |
| IV–V | >90 | **Admit** — refer to ED immediately |

### Outpatient Treatment Algorithm
1. **No comorbidities, no antibiotic use in past 3 months**:
   - Amoxicillin 1 g TID × 5 days *(Tier 1)*
   - Alternative if penicillin allergy: Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 5 days

2. **With comorbidities** (DM, CKD, heart failure, COPD, immunosuppression):
   - Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg BID × 5 days PLUS Azithromycin 500 mg day 1, 250 mg days 2–5
   - Alternative (respiratory fluoroquinolone): Levofloxacin 750 mg QD × 5 days (requires PA)

3. **Suspected atypical** (Mycoplasma, Chlamydophila, Legionella — gradual onset, dry cough, headache):
   - Azithromycin 500 mg day 1, then 250 mg QD days 2–5
   - Alternative: Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 5 days

### Mandatory Orders
- [ ] Chest X-ray PA + Lateral (or portable AP if mobility limited)
- [ ] CBC with differential
- [ ] BMP (Na, K, BUN, Creatinine)
- [ ] Blood cultures × 2 ONLY if hospitalizing
- [ ] SpO2 at rest and with ambulation
- [ ] Sputum culture if purulent sputum

### Follow-up
- Phone check at 48–72 hours to assess response
- In-person follow-up in 4–6 weeks for repeat CXR to confirm resolution
- **Return to ED criteria** (provide written instructions): SpO2 < 92%, respiratory rate > 25, altered mental status, inability to maintain oral intake

## PROTOCOL 2: Acute Gastroenteritis (AGE) — Management

### Assessment
- Duration, frequency of vomiting/diarrhea, blood in stool, fever grade
- Exposure history: travel, food history, sick contacts, daycare/institutional exposure
- **Red flags requiring immediate referral**: bloody diarrhea, high fever (>38.9°C/102°F), signs of dehydration (≥5% weight loss, tachycardia, dry mucosa, decreased urine output), immunocompromised

### Hydration Assessment (Dehydration Scoring)
| Grade | Clinical Signs | Action |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| None | Normal | PO hydration, home management |
| Mild (<3%) | Slightly dry mucosa | ORS 50 mL/kg over 4h |
| Moderate (3–9%) | Dry mucosa, reduced skin turgor, tachycardia | ORS 100 mL/kg over 4h; reassess 2h |
| Severe (>9%) | Sunken eyes, no tears, lethargic | IV fluids — refer to ED |

### Treatment
- **Viral gastroenteritis** (most common; adenovirus, norovirus, rotavirus):
  - Oral rehydration therapy (ORT) with WHO-ORS or equivalent (Pedialyte)
  - BRAT diet transition as tolerated
  - Probiotics (Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG): optional, may reduce duration by 1 day
  - **NO antibiotics** for suspected viral etiology

- **Bacterial gastroenteritis** (Salmonella non-typhi, Campylobacter — usually self-limiting):
  - Antibiotics NOT routinely recommended for immunocompetent adults
  - Treat if: febrile, bloody diarrhea, immunocompromised, or bacteremia suspected
  - Empiric: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg BID × 3–5 days OR Azithromycin 500 mg QD × 3 days

- **Antiemetics** (for symptom relief in adults):
  - Ondansetron 4–8 mg PO/ODT q8h PRN (preferred; requires PA for age <4)
  - Prochlorperazine 10 mg PO q6h PRN (alternative)

- **Antidiarrheals**:
  - Loperamide 4 mg initial dose, then 2 mg after each loose stool (max 16 mg/day)
  - **Avoid** loperamide if bloody diarrhea or suspected invasive bacterial infection

### Mandatory Orders
- [ ] Basic metabolic panel if moderate/severe dehydration
- [ ] Stool culture + O&P if: duration >7 days, bloody diarrhea, travel history, immunocompromised
- [ ] CBC if febrile with diarrhea

## PROTOCOL 3: Hypertension — Initial Evaluation and Management

### Diagnosis
- Stage 1 HTN: SBP 130–139 OR DBP 80–89 mmHg (confirmed on ≥2 visits)
- Stage 2 HTN: SBP ≥140 OR DBP ≥90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Urgency: SBP >180 OR DBP >120, no end-organ damage → treat urgently, do not send to ED unless symptoms
- Hypertensive Emergency: SBP >180 OR DBP >120 WITH end-organ damage → **call 911 / transfer ED immediately**

### Initial Workup (All New HTN Diagnoses)
- [ ] BMP (K+, creatinine, glucose)
- [ ] Urinalysis with micro
- [ ] Lipid panel (fasting)
- [ ] ECG (12-lead)
- [ ] CBC
- [ ] TSH if clinically indicated

### Treatment Algorithm
1. **Lifestyle modification first** (Stage 1 without CVD risk):
   - DASH diet, sodium < 2.3 g/day, weight loss, ≥150 min/week moderate exercise, limit alcohol, smoking cessation
   - Re-evaluate in 3–6 months

2. **Pharmacotherapy indicated** (Stage 2, or Stage 1 with DM/CKD/CVD):
   - No compelling indications: Thiazide (HCTZ 12.5–25 mg QD) or CCB (amlodipine 5 mg QD) or ACE-I (lisinopril 10 mg QD)
   - With DM or CKD: **ACE-I first-line** (lisinopril 10 mg QD); monitor K+, Cr at 2 weeks
   - With HFrEF: ACE-I + beta-blocker (refer to cardiology)
   - African-American patients: Thiazide + CCB preferred over ACE-I monotherapy

### Follow-up
- Repeat BP in 2–4 weeks after medication initiation
- Target BP: <130/80 for most adults; <140/90 for elderly (≥65 frail)
- Labs every 6–12 months once stable

## PROTOCOL 4: Type 2 Diabetes — Initial Management and Monitoring

### Diagnosis
- FPG ≥ 126 mg/dL (fasting ≥8h) — confirm with repeat test
- 2h OGTT ≥ 200 mg/dL
- HbA1c ≥ 6.5% — confirm with repeat test
- Random glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL + symptoms

### Initial Workup
- [ ] HbA1c
- [ ] Fasting lipid panel
- [ ] BMP (creatinine, K+, glucose)
- [ ] Urinalysis + urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR)
- [ ] eGFR calculation
- [ ] ECG
- [ ] Foot exam (sensory testing)
- [ ] Dilated eye exam referral

### Treatment Algorithm
1. Lifestyle modification: Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT) + 150 min/week exercise; refer to diabetes educator
2. **Metformin first-line** if eGFR ≥ 30: Start 500 mg BID with meals, titrate to 1000 mg BID over 4 weeks
3. Add second agent if HbA1c > 9% or uncontrolled at 3 months:
   - With ASCVD/CKD: GLP-1 agonist (semaglutide) or SGLT-2 inhibitor (empagliflozin) — requires PA
   - No CVD risk factors: SGLT-2 or DPP-4 inhibitor (sitagliptin)
4. Insulin referral if HbA1c > 10% or symptomatic hyperglycemia

### Monitoring Schedule (Stable T2DM)
| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| HbA1c | Every 3 months until target, then every 6 months |
| BMP (K+, Cr) | Every 6 months |
| Urine UACR | Annually |
| Lipid panel | Annually |
| Foot exam | Every visit |
| Eye exam | Annually |
| Dental | Every 6 months |

### Targets
- HbA1c: < 7.0% (< 8.0% for elderly/frail/limited life expectancy)
- BP: < 130/80 mmHg
- LDL: < 70 mg/dL if ASCVD; < 100 mg/dL otherwise

## PROTOCOL 5: Fever in Adults — Evaluation

### Assessment
- Fever defined as: oral temperature ≥ 38.0°C (100.4°F)
- Hyperpyrexia: ≥ 41.0°C — medical emergency

### Key History Points
- Duration, pattern, associated symptoms
- Travel history (malaria, dengue, typhoid risk)
- Immunosuppression (HIV, steroids, chemotherapy)
- Recent sick contacts, animal exposure
- Medication history (drug fever)

### Common Etiologies by Symptom Pattern
| Pattern | Consider |
|---------|----------|
| Fever + productive cough + infiltrate | CAP → see Protocol 1 |
| Fever + dysuria + flank pain | Pyelonephritis |
| Fever + sore throat + exudate | Strep/Mono → rapid strep, monospot |
| Fever + diarrhea/vomiting | AGE → see Protocol 2 |
| Fever + rash + travel | Dengue, Zika, Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever |
| Fever + RUQ pain | Cholecystitis → ultrasound + surgical consult |
| Fever + RLQ pain + nausea | Appendicitis → refer ED for CT abdomen |
| Fever >10 days no source | Occult bacteremia, lymphoma → CBC, CMP, CT |

### Antipyretic Therapy
- Acetaminophen 1000 mg q6h OR Ibuprofen 400–600 mg q6h (if no contraindications)
- Alternate acetaminophen and ibuprofen q3h for refractory fever
- **Do NOT use aspirin** for fever in patients < 18 (Reye syndrome risk)
