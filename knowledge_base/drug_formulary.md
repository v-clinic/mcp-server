# vClinic Drug Formulary — Approved Medications List
## Revision: 2026-01 | Approved by: Pharmacy & Therapeutics Committee

---

## ANTIBIOTICS

### Amoxicillin (Oral)
- **Forms**: 250 mg/5 mL suspension; 500 mg capsules; 875 mg tablets
- **Adult dosing**:
  - Community-acquired pneumonia (mild, outpatient): 1 g TID × 5–7 days
  - Strep pharyngitis: 500 mg BID × 10 days
  - UTI (uncomplicated): 500 mg TID × 7 days
- **Pediatric dosing**: 40–90 mg/kg/day divided TID (max 3 g/day)
- **Contraindications**: Penicillin hypersensitivity
- **Notes**: Preferred first-line for CAP in patients without comorbidities per local resistance data (S. pneumoniae resistance <10%)

### Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin)
- **Forms**: 875/125 mg tablets; 600/42.9 mg/5 mL suspension
- **Adult dosing**:
  - AECB / sinusitis: 875/125 mg BID × 5–7 days
  - Skin/soft tissue: 875/125 mg BID × 7–10 days
  - Bite wounds: 875/125 mg BID × 5 days
- **Contraindications**: Penicillin allergy; history of cholestatic jaundice with amoxicillin-clavulanate
- **Formulary tier**: Preferred

### Azithromycin (Z-Pack)
- **Forms**: 250 mg, 500 mg tablets; 200 mg/5 mL suspension
- **Adult dosing**:
  - CAP atypical (outpatient): 500 mg day 1, then 250 mg QD days 2–5
  - Chlamydia: 1 g single dose
  - MAC prophylaxis: 1.2 g weekly
- **Formulary tier**: Preferred for atypical organisms; second-line for CAP in penicillin allergy
- **Note**: QTc monitoring if concurrent QT-prolonging drugs

### Doxycycline
- **Forms**: 100 mg capsules
- **Adult dosing**:
  - CAP atypical: 100 mg BID × 5 days
  - Lyme disease: 100 mg BID × 10–21 days
  - MRSA skin/soft tissue: 100 mg BID × 5–7 days
- **Contraindications**: Pregnancy, children < 8 years
- **Notes**: Take with full glass of water; avoid lying down 30 min after dose

### Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX / Bactrim)
- **Forms**: 80/400 mg (SS), 160/800 mg (DS) tablets
- **Adult dosing**:
  - Uncomplicated UTI: 1 DS BID × 3 days (use only if local E. coli resistance <20%)
  - MRSA soft tissue: 1–2 DS BID × 5–7 days
  - PCP prophylaxis: 1 SS QD
- **Contraindications**: Sulfa allergy; G6PD deficiency; pregnancy at term
- **Local resistance data**: E. coli TMP-SMX resistance at vClinic catchment area: 18% (2025 antibiogram)

### Ciprofloxacin
- **Forms**: 250 mg, 500 mg tablets
- **Adult dosing**:
  - Uncomplicated UTI: 250 mg BID × 3 days
  - Complicated UTI / pyelonephritis: 500 mg BID × 7 days
  - GI infections: 500 mg BID × 5–7 days
- **Restrictions**: Reserve for organisms confirmed susceptible or penicillin/cephalosporin failure
- **Notes**: Avoid in children; black box — tendinopathy risk

## ANALGESICS & ANTI-INFLAMMATORIES

### Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
- **Forms**: 325 mg, 500 mg tablets; 160 mg/5 mL liquid
- **Adult dosing**: 500–1000 mg q6h PRN (max 4 g/day; max 2 g/day if liver disease or heavy alcohol use)
- **Pediatric dosing**: 10–15 mg/kg q4–6h PRN
- **Formulary tier**: First-line analgesic/antipyretic

### Ibuprofen (Advil / Motrin)
- **Forms**: 200 mg OTC; 400 mg, 600 mg, 800 mg Rx tablets
- **Adult dosing**: 400–800 mg TID-QID with food (max 3.2 g/day Rx; 1.2 g/day OTC)
- **Contraindications**: Active peptic ulcer, CrCl < 30 mL/min, NSAID hypersensitivity, third trimester pregnancy
- **Notes**: Add PPI (omeprazole 20 mg QD) for patients >65 or with GI risk factors

### Naproxen
- **Forms**: 250 mg, 375 mg, 500 mg tablets
- **Adult dosing**: 250–500 mg BID (max 1.25 g/day)
- **Notes**: Longer duration than ibuprofen; preferred for gout flares and dysmenorrhea

## ACID SUPPRESSANTS

### Omeprazole
- **Forms**: 20 mg, 40 mg capsules (delayed release)
- **Adult dosing**:
  - GERD: 20 mg QD × 4–8 weeks
  - H. pylori (with antibiotics): 20 mg BID × 10–14 days
  - GI prophylaxis with NSAIDs: 20 mg QD
- **Formulary tier**: Preferred PPI

---

## RESPIRATORY

### Albuterol (Salbutamol) Inhaler
- **Forms**: MDI 90 mcg/actuation; 2.5 mg/3 mL nebulizer solution
- **Adult dosing**:
  - Acute bronchospasm: 2 puffs q4–6h PRN
  - Pre-exercise: 2 puffs 15 min before activity
- **Notes**: Provide spacer for all MDI prescriptions; coach proper technique

### Fluticasone Inhaled Corticosteroid
- **Forms**: 44 mcg, 110 mcg, 220 mcg/actuation MDI
- **Adult dosing (asthma)**:
  - Mild persistent: 88 mcg BID
  - Moderate persistent: 88–220 mcg BID
  - Severe persistent: 220 mcg BID + LABA referral
- **Notes**: Rinse mouth after use; step up if uncontrolled on current dose

## ANTIHYPERTENSIVES

### Lisinopril (ACE Inhibitor)
- **Forms**: 5 mg, 10 mg, 20 mg, 40 mg tablets
- **Adult dosing (HTN)**: Start 10 mg QD; titrate to 20–40 mg QD
- **Adult dosing (HFrEF)**: Start 2.5–5 mg QD; target 20–40 mg QD
- **Contraindications**: Pregnancy, bilateral renal artery stenosis, prior angioedema with ACE-I
- **Monitoring**: BMP at 1–2 weeks after initiation or dose change (K+, Cr)
- **Formulary tier**: Preferred for HTN + DM, HTN + CKD

### Amlodipine (Calcium Channel Blocker)
- **Forms**: 2.5 mg, 5 mg, 10 mg tablets
- **Adult dosing**: 5 mg QD; may increase to 10 mg QD after 7–14 days
- **Notes**: Peripheral edema common; preferred add-on if ACE-I alone insufficient

### Hydrochlorothiazide (Thiazide Diuretic)
- **Forms**: 12.5 mg, 25 mg tablets
- **Adult dosing**: 12.5–25 mg QD
- **Monitoring**: BMP annually (electrolytes, glucose, uric acid)

---

## DIABETES

### Metformin
- **Forms**: 500 mg, 850 mg, 1000 mg tablets; 500 mg/5 mL solution
- **Adult dosing**: Start 500 mg BID with meals; titrate to 1000 mg BID (max 2.55 g/day)
- **Contraindications**: eGFR < 30; hold 48h before/after IV contrast
- **Formulary tier**: First-line for type 2 DM

---

## FORMULARY TIERS
- **Tier 1 (Preferred/Generic)**: Lowest copay — amoxicillin, TMP-SMX, acetaminophen, ibuprofen, omeprazole, lisinopril, metformin, albuterol MDI
- **Tier 2 (Preferred Brand)**: Moderate copay — amoxicillin-clavulanate, azithromycin, fluticasone, amlodipine
- **Tier 3 (Non-preferred)**: Highest copay — ciprofloxacin (restricted use), doxycycline
- **Prior Authorization Required**: All biologics, specialty medications, and IV antibiotics
